\section{Truco Paulista as a POSG}

We formalize Truco Paulista as a two-player Partially Observable Stochastic Game (POSG), defined by the tuple $\langle \mathcal{N}, \mathcal{S}, \{\mathcal{A}_i\}, \{\mathcal{O}_i\}, T, O, R \rangle$.

\subsection{Players and State Space}

\textbf{Players:} $\mathcal{N} = \{1, 2\}$.

\textbf{State space} $\mathcal{S}$ comprises:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Deck state:} The 40-card deck permutation and deal assignment.
    \item \textbf{Hands:} $h_i \subseteq \mathcal{C}$, where $|\mathcal{C}| = 40$ and $|h_i| \leq 3$.
    \item \textbf{Vira:} The revealed community card $v \in \mathcal{C}$ that determines the manilha rank.
    \item \textbf{Trick history:} The sequence of cards played in the current round, $\tau = [(c_1^{(t)}, c_2^{(t)})]_{t=1}^{k}$ for $k \leq 3$ tricks.
    \item \textbf{Escalation state:} $(l, p, r) \in \mathcal{L} \times (\mathcal{N} \cup \{\bot\}) \times (\mathcal{N} \cup \{\bot\})$, where $l$ is the current level, $p$ is a pending request, and $r$ is the last player to escalate.
    \item \textbf{Game score:} $(s_1, s_2) \in \{0, \ldots, 12\}^2$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Observation Space}

Player $i$'s observation $o_i \in \mathcal{O}_i$ includes:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Their own hand $h_i$
    \item The vira card $v$
    \item The number of cards in the opponent's hand $|h_{-i}|$
    \item The trick history $\tau$ (all played cards are public)
    \item The escalation state $(l, p, r)$
    \item The game score $(s_1, s_2)$
\end{itemize}

Crucially, player $i$ \emph{never} observes the opponent's hand $h_{-i}$, making this a game of imperfect information.

\subsection{Action Space}

The action space $\mathcal{A}_i$ is context-dependent:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Card play:} $\texttt{PLAY\_CARD}(j)$ for $j \in \{0, \ldots, |h_i|-1\}$
    \item \textbf{Escalation:} $\texttt{TRUCO}$ (initiate or implied by context)
    \item \textbf{Response:} $\texttt{ACCEPT}$, $\texttt{RAISE}$, $\texttt{FOLD}$
\end{itemize}
